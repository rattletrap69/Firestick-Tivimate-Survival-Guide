Frequently Asked Questions

How does the subscription and billing actually work?

Typically, when your IT guy or provider sets you up for the very first time, you pay a one-time upfront cost (usually around $180). This covers the physical hardware (the Firestick), his time setting up all the necessary apps, and your first 6 months of live TV service.

After your first 6 months are up, the ongoing subscription is usually around $150 every 6 months. Your provider will let you know when it is time to renew!

What is the difference between the default TV app (EZTV) and TiviMate?

When you get your stick, it usually comes pre-loaded with a default app (like EZTV or Smarters). This app works fine and gets you access to all your live channels and movies.

However, TiviMate is considered the absolute gold-standard upgrade. It is a premium, third-party "guide" app that takes your provider's channels and organizes them beautifully. It makes your IPTV look, feel, and act exactly like a high-end cable or satellite box. If you want the best experience, you want TiviMate.

Do I have to pay extra for TiviMate? How do I get it?

Yes. Because TiviMate is created by an independent software developer (not your TV provider), it requires its own separate license.

To upgrade, you must download the "TiviMate Companion" app onto an Android phone or tablet from the Google Play Store. In that app, you create an account and pay a one-time Lifetime Premium fee (usually around $30). Once you pay that one-time fee, you own TiviMate for life! You can then use this guide (Step 4 of the Emergency Fix) to log into the app on your TV.

If I remove my friend's Amazon account, will it delete my TV apps?

NO! This is 100% safe.

A lot of people are terrified that clicking "Deregister" on the Amazon account will factory reset their device and wipe out all their IPTV settings. It does not. Deregistering only signs you out of Amazon's official store and Prime Video. Sideloaded apps like TiviMate and your IPTV playlists are completely untouched. (See the "Firestick" tab for safe instructions on how to switch accounts!)

Why can't I just record TV directly onto my Firestick?

Streaming sticks like the Firestick or Chromecast are designed to stream video from the internet, not store it. They typically only have about 4 to 8 gigabytes of total storage space, most of which is taken up by the operating system.

A single hour of High-Definition Live TV takes up about 4 gigabytes of space. If you try to hit "Record" without a USB drive plugged in, the Firestick will instantly run out of memory, crash, and possibly delete your apps to save itself. You must use an OTG cable and a USB Flash Drive to record.

Why Optimize TiviMate?

IPTV services provide massive amounts of data (thousands of live channels and movies). Out of the box, TiviMate isn't perfectly set up to handle this much data smoothly. Following these tutorials will stop buffering, fix your guide, and make the app feel like a premium cable box.

The Top 5 Tweaks to Make Immediately

1. Increase the Buffer Size

Fixes Buffering

The Benefit: Changing this setting forces TiviMate to download a few extra seconds of live TV ahead of time. If your internet briefly blips, the video won't freeze.

  1. Go to ⚙️ Settings > Playback.
  2. Scroll down to Buffer Size.
  3. Change it from "Normal" to Large or Max.

2. Hide Unwanted Countries & Groups

Cleans TV Guide

The Benefit: Your provider gives you channels from all over the world. If you only speak English, there is no reason to load 500 Italian channels. Hiding them makes your guide load 10x faster.

  1. Open the left side menu so you see the list of channel categories (like "USA Entertainment").
  2. Find a category you don't want (like "UK Sports").
  3. Press and hold the 🔘 Select button for 2 seconds.
  4. In the menu that pops up on the right, click Hide.
  5. *Note: If you ever want them back, go to Settings > Playlists > Manage Groups to unhide them.

3. Turn on Auto Frame Rate (AFR)

Smoother Sports

The Benefit: Some TV channels broadcast at 60 frames per second, some at 50, and movies at 24. This setting tells your TV to perfectly match the broadcast, preventing the picture from looking "jerky" when a football is thrown or a camera pans across a landscape.

  1. Go to ⚙️ Settings > Playback.
  2. Select Auto frame rate (AFR).
  3. Turn it ON.

4. Automate the TV Guide (EPG)

No More Blank Guides

The Benefit: The "Electronic Program Guide" (EPG) is the data that shows you what show is playing at what time. By default, it doesn't update often enough. This forces it to download fresh data the second you turn on the TV.

  1. Go to ⚙️ Settings > EPG.
  2. Turn Update on app start to ON.
  3. Change Update interval to 24 Hours (keeps things fresh without overloading your internet).

5. Setup Your Favorites List

Quality of Life

The Benefit: Stop scrolling through 3,000 channels to find your 10 favorite news and sports stations. Build your own custom category!

  1. Highlight a channel you watch often in the TV guide.
  2. Press and hold the 🔘 Select button for 2 seconds.
  3. A menu pops up on the right. Click Add to Favorites (a little star will appear).
  4. To view them later: Press ⬅️ Left to open the menu, scroll to the very top, and select Favorites.

Everyday Fixes

TV Guide is completely blank

If your guide says "No Information" everywhere, the hidden data file got corrupted. Let's nuke it and start over.

  1. Go to ⚙️ Settings > EPG.
  2. Scroll to the very bottom, click Clear EPG.
  3. Once cleared, scroll up and click Update EPG. Wait 3 full minutes.

The Audio is Out of Sync

If people's lips are moving but the sound doesn't match:

  1. While watching the channel, press 🔘 Select to pop up the bottom menu.
  2. Scroll right and select the Audio Sync icon.
  3. Tap left or right to adjust the milliseconds until the sound matches the lips.

How to watch a past show (Catch-up)

If you missed the news yesterday, you can often rewind time.

  • Look in the TV guide. Does the channel name have a tiny 🕒 Clock Icon next to it?
  • If yes, simply press and hold ⬅️ Left to scroll back into yesterday's schedule.
  • Click on any past show to watch it like a recorded movie.

How to turn on Closed Captions

For live TV or Movies on Demand.

  • While watching, press 🔘 Select to open the bottom menu.
  • Scroll right to the CC icon and click it.
  • Choose the English subtitle track.

Community Reddit Hacks

User-Agent Spoofing (ISP Blocks)

Fixes: HTTP 401/403 Errors, or ISPs throttling you because they recognize the TiviMate signature.

  1. Go to ⚙️ Settings > General > User-Agent.
  2. Type exactly: wink/1.31.1 or IPTVSmartersPro
  3. Click Done. This disguises your traffic to the host server.

Beating ISP Deep Packet Throttling

Fixes: Perfect TV all day, but massive buffering at 7 PM or during big sporting events.

  • ISPs actively deploy Deep Packet Inspection to throttle UDP/TCP video streams during peak hours.
  • You must install a VPN (Surfshark, NordVPN, etc.) directly on the device. An encrypted tunnel blinds the ISP to your video data payload.

External Player & Manual Subtitles

Fixes: VOD Movies crashing, lacking audio tracks, or missing Closed Captions.

  1. Install VLC or MX Player from the App Store.
  2. In TiviMate, go to Settings > Playback > Use External Player > Set to VOD Only.
  3. Missing Subs? Download the `.srt` file from opensubtitles.com via Downloader, then load it manually using MX Player's "Subtitle > Open" menu.

HW/SW Decoding

Fixes: Black screen with perfect audio.

  1. Go to ⚙️ Settings > Playback > Audio/Video Decoder.
  2. Change it from Hardware (HW) to Software (SW). This bypasses a failing hardware chip and uses raw software processing.

Why Optimize the Firestick?

Out of the box, Amazon Firesticks are programmed to show you as many ads as possible, track your data, and auto-play videos on the home screen. All of these background tasks eat up your device's memory and internet speed—resources that your Live TV app desperately needs. By turning these off, we make the stick faster and more reliable.

The Top Firestick Speed Hacks

1. Stop Auto-Playing Ads on the Home Screen

The Benefit: Have you noticed your Firestick gets super laggy right when you turn on the TV? It's because Amazon immediately tries to download and play a high-definition movie trailer ad at the top of the screen. Turning this off stops the lag.

  1. Go to ⚙️ Settings (gear icon on the home screen).
  2. Select Preferences.
  3. Select Featured Content.
  4. Turn Allow Video Autoplay to OFF.
  5. Turn Allow Audio Autoplay to OFF.

2. Turn Off Amazon's Data Tracking

The Benefit: Amazon constantly tracks every button you press to build a profile on you. This process runs in the background 24/7, stealing your processing power. Turning it off protects your privacy and speeds up the device.

  1. Go to ⚙️ Settings > Preferences.
  2. Select Privacy Settings.
  3. Turn Device Usage Data to OFF.
  4. Turn Collect App Usage Data to OFF.

3. Stop Background Notifications

The Benefit: Nothing is worse than watching a tense movie and having a giant banner pop up saying "Try Amazon Music!". This silences all pop-ups.

  1. Go to ⚙️ Settings > Preferences.
  2. Select Notification Settings.
  3. Click Do Not Interrupt so it changes to ON.

Switching Amazon Accounts (Without Losing TiviMate)

100% SAFE: De-registering an Amazon account does NOT delete side-loaded apps like TiviMate or wipe your IPTV settings. It only signs out of Amazon services (like Prime Video).

If your friend or provider left their Amazon account logged into your Firestick, you can safely remove it and add your own personal account by following these exact steps:

  1. Go to the ⚙️ Gear Icon (Settings) on the far right of the Firestick Home Screen.
  2. Select Account & Profile Settings.
  3. Click on Amazon Account.
  4. Select Deregister, and then click Deregister again to confirm.
  5. The Firestick will briefly refresh and ask you to log in. Enter your own Amazon Email and Password.
  6. Once logged in, open TiviMate. Everything will be exactly as you left it!

Force Restart / Unfreeze

If the Firestick is completely frozen and you can't reach behind the TV to unplug it, use this secret remote shortcut:

Press and hold both the 🔘 Center Button AND the ⏯️ Play/Pause button at the exact same time for 10 seconds. The screen will go black and the Firestick will reboot itself.

Hardware Requirements

Streaming devices have minimal storage (1 hour of HD = 4GB). You must add external storage to record live TV.

  • OTG Cable / Adapter: Splits the power port so you can plug a USB drive into the Firestick.
  • USB Flash Drive: Must be formatted to FAT32.

Bypassing Android OS Recording Blocks

Modern Android/FireOS actively blocks external recording. You must use one of these workarounds to give TiviMate permission to save files:

Hack 1: The Android Folder Trick

If you get a "You don't have an app that can do this" error:

  1. Install a File Manager (like X-plore).
  2. Open your USB Drive, go to Android > media.
  3. Create a new folder named EXACTLY: ar.tvplayer.tv
  4. Go back to TiviMate > Settings > Recording, and select that specific folder. The OS grants implicit permission to it.
Hack 2: FireOS 8 System Picker Broken

On FireOS 8, the graphical OK button is hidden. Use an ADB app on your phone to force the keystroke:

  1. Open the broken folder screen on the TV.
  2. Connect via "Remote ADB Shell" app on your phone.
  3. Inject this exact code to force the hidden button:
input keyevent 61 && input keyevent 66 && input keyevent 61 && input keyevent 62 dpad
